How does it work?


So, how does any of this work? Well, allow me to give a superficial explanation on the inner workings of this planetary jukebox.

Firstly, using Astropy's astroquery package, we fine-tune our celestial guitar by tapping into NASA's JPL Horizons. This grants us access to data on the planets' positions relative to the sun. Once we secure these celestial coordinates, we neatly categorize each planet into one of nine nonants, each representing a unique pitch in the Scientific Pitch Notation.

With pitches assigned, we kickstart the cosmic orchestra by associating each planet its corresponding note and pitch through frequency modulation. Given that each planet represents a note, it's just a matter of tweaking the frequency that brings us to the right pitch. Furthermore, to achieve the collective harmony of all planets combined, we obtain the combined tone that all the planets play together by calculating the average difference between all the frequencies of the planets.

Finally, to visualize this celestial melody, we position the planets on our canvas. We utilize the arctangent of their initial positions within their corresponding orbits; and, factoring in their orbit size and sector information, we obtain their current sector location. Allowing us to choreograph their cosmic dance in pixel-perfect harmony.
